**The Gallery** is an interactive virtual museum built entirely with HTML, CSS, and Vanilla JavaScript — no frameworks, no libraries, no game engines.

Instead of a conventional portfolio page, it invites you to *step inside*. Walk through handcrafted gallery rooms, approach framed artwork, and experience each piece as though it were hanging on a real museum wall — your small companion dog trotting right alongside you.

---

## The Idea

Most portfolio websites ask visitors to scroll.

I wanted mine to be explored.

The Gallery started with a single question: *What if a portfolio felt like visiting an art museum instead of browsing a webpage?* Every interaction was designed around that. You don't jump between pages — you walk through hallways. You don't browse thumbnails — you approach framed artwork. Each room has its own architecture, lighting, and atmosphere, making every collection feel like a distinct exhibition rather than just another section.

---

## Rooms

### Room I — The Grand Hall
A warm, classical gallery lit by soft golden spotlights and candlelit wall sconces. Rich wooden interiors, velvet barriers, and floating orbs set the scene for a collection of traditional paintings.

### Room II — The Hall of Constellations
Step through the archway and the warmth gives way to midnight blue. Stars shimmer overhead, golden astronomical diagrams trace the dome, and obsidian floors reflect the cosmos beneath your feet. This room houses a collection of digital illustrations — each piece glowing quietly against the celestial dark.

The shift between these two spaces isn't just visual. It mirrors the contrast between traditional and digital art, each given an environment built around it.

### Room III — The Royal Opera
A grand theatre complete with chandeliers, velvet curtains, and tiered balconies. Tap the stage to begin — the curtains part, the spotlight falls, and the story of who I am unfolds in a slow cinematic credits roll. Side walls hold framed achievements and moments from my college art society, Kraftovity.

## Features

- Walkable side-scrolling rooms with smooth camera follow
- Three fully realised exhibition spaces, each with a unique aesthetic
- Walking character and companion dog with idle animations
- Click any frame to open an artwork viewer with description
- Cinematic Room III curtain-open + credits sequence
- Atmospheric lighting — spotlights, halos, sconce flames, floating orbs
- Shooting stars and sparkle bursts in Room II
- Mobile-friendly with on-screen movement controls
- Keyboard navigation (Arrow keys / A / D)
- `prefers-reduced-motion` support for accessibility
- Zero dependencies — pure HTML, CSS, and JavaScript

---

## Controls

| Action | Control |
|---|---|
| Walk in hallway | Click anywhere on the floor |
| Enter a room | Click a door |
| Move inside a room | Arrow Keys or **A / D** |
| Inspect an artwork | Click the frame |
| Close artwork | **Esc** or **✕** |
| Exit a room | Walk to the archway |
| Mobile | On-screen ◀ ▶ buttons |
